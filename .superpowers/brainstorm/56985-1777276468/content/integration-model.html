How should intelligence signals connect to trading?

This is the most important architectural decision — it shapes everything else we build.

A

Filter Layer

Intelligence acts as a gate on existing strategy signals. If congressional sentiment is bearish on a sector, ORB / VWAP signals for that sector are suppressed. If a key figure's news is bullish, risk limits loosen slightly.

Pros

  • Least code change — strategies stay intact
  • Easy to audit: "this trade was blocked by political filter"
  • Gradual rollout possible

Cons

  • Intelligence can only suppress, not initiate
  • May over-filter on noisy signals
B

Intelligence Dashboard (Manual)

A dedicated research page shows congressional trades, news sentiment, social mood, and AI analysis in one view. You read it and decide manually — no automatic feed into live trading.

Pros

  • Zero automation risk
  • Full human control
  • Fastest to ship — no strategy changes

Cons

  • Doesn't scale — manual review required before every session
  • Doesn't leverage the edge automatically
C

AI Signal Generator

Intelligence layers generate their own independent trade signals (buy/sell/neutral per ticker + sector) that flow through the existing signal allocator alongside strategy signals. The AI's conviction score competes with strategy confidence scores.

Pros

  • Most powerful — intelligence can initiate trades
  • Fully automated alpha
  • Composable with all existing risk controls

Cons

  • Most complex to build and test safely
  • Requires thorough backtesting of AI signals before live use
  • LLM latency adds delay to signal generation

My recommendation

A + B together. Build the dashboard first (B) so you can see and trust the signals before automating. Then wire it as a filter layer (A). Save full signal generation (C) for after the signals are validated in production.
